Charmoula: More than a marinade, charmoula is a beloved base in Moroccan kitchens,
vibrant blend used as sauce, condiment, or seasoning for everything from grilled fish to
roasted vegetables. Made with garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, fresh herbs, and spices,
charmoula varies from region to region and even from one family to another.
Couscous: Made from coarsely ground durum wheat (semolina).The semolina is steamed in
a couscoussière four times over cabbage peel–infused water. Between each steam, the
grains are moistened, gently separated by hand, and enriched with sunflower oil, salt,
butter, smen, and black pepper; to ensure the couscous is perfectly airy and tender.
Couscoussière: Traditional steamer with two chambers: the lower pot simmers meats or
vegetables, while the upper part, called a couscoussière, steams couscous, allowing it to
absorb the rising aromatic vapours through tiny holes in its base.
Harissa: Harissa sauce is a traditional Moroccan condiment, typically made by grinding
fresh garlic and dried chilies in a mortar and pestle, along with spices and olive oil. In this
version, we combine a generous amount of mild fresh chilies, balancing the flavour with our
home made preserved lemons, lemon juice, olive oil and a touch of salt.
Οrganic saffron from Taliouine: Our Taliouine saffron comes from a cooperative of 20
families who cultivate it at 1,200 meters in the remote, dry peaks of the Anti-Atlas
Mountains. Established in the early 2000s, the cooperative was created to improve the
living and working conditions of local farmers. Known as Morocco’s “Red Gold,” the saffron
is hand-harvested in the sun-drenched fields of Taliouine. Thousands of delicate purple
flowers are carefully picked, with each containing three vibrant red stigmas, which are
meticulously separated by hand to ensure only the finest saffron.
