The food on Koh Phangan has changed a great deal over the past few years. You can still eat excellent Thai food for very little, but you will also find chef-led tasting menus, a Michelin-recognised garden kitchen, Italian trattorias, Japanese teppanyaki and beachfront tables built around the sunset.

That range is part of the pleasure — and part of the difficulty. With so many options spread across the island, choosing where to eat can be surprisingly hard.

This guide brings together some of the restaurants we return to and hear about most often, grouped by cuisine so you can find what you are in the mood for, wherever you are staying. It is written from the island, by people who live here through the year rather than for a single holiday.

## How We Selected These Restaurants

This guide is published by Dar Mansour, a Moroccan restaurant in Hin Kong. Our own restaurant is included where it genuinely fits the subject, but the wider selection is based on food quality, character, atmosphere, value and usefulness for the reader — not on commercial partnerships. We are one option among many, and the list is more useful because of that.

We looked at consistency, a clear sense of identity, the setting and how well each place suits the occasion it is known for. Koh Phangan is a small island where restaurants open, move and change hands often, so treat this as a living guide: **hours change with the season — always check current times on Google before you go**, using the map link under each entry.

## Thai &amp; Local

### Mama KOP

A family-run Thai kitchen right on Hin Kong Beach, and the beachfront branch of the well-loved original in Sri Thanu. The cooking is home-style and honest — pad thai, penang and massaman curries, khao soi, papaya salad — at prices that stay refreshingly low. Tables sit almost on the sand, the welcome is warm, and the staff genuinely understand vegan requests.

**Why we love it —** proper home-style Thai food with your feet almost in the sand, and no tourist mark-up.
**Good to know —** ฿฿ · walk-ins usually fine · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** directly on Hin Kong Beach, a short stroll south of L'Alcove. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Mama+KOP+Koh+Phangan)

### Dear Phangan

A Michelin-recognised garden restaurant that works to a daily blind menu — no fixed dishes, just whatever the chef brings back from the pier and market that morning, alongside fruit and herbs from the garden. Past highlights have run from crab and water-chestnut cakes to grilled fish in banana leaf and squid in its own ink, and the Thai owner talks you through each course and its provenance. Set in a wooden Thai house lit by fairy lights, it feels a world away from the party side of the island. Booking is essential, as only a set number of covers are prepared each night.

**Why we love it —** a daily surprise menu built entirely on what came off the boat that morning.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · reservations essential, opens from early evening · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** just inland from Hin Kong Beach, a few minutes north of Amsterdam Bar and close to Tum Canal Viewpoint. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Dear+Phangan+restaurant)

### Sonolito Thai Food

A relaxed local kitchen serving authentic home-style Thai food in a simple setting. Generous portions, fresh ingredients and honest flavours make it the kind of place people discover by chance and return to through their stay.

**Why we love it —** the flavours you came to Thailand for, without any fuss or markup.
**Good to know —** ฿ · walk-ins fine · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** on the main road between Hin Kong and Sri Thanu, a little north of Indigo Specialty Coffee & Bakery. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Sonolito+Thai+Food+Koh+Phangan)

### Thai Element

Traditional Thai flavours reworked with a lighter, more modern touch, and a genuinely vegan-friendly menu. A good choice when part of the table wants classic Thai and part wants something plant-based.

**Why we love it —** familiar Thai dishes with a fresh, contemporary edge.
**Good to know —** ฿฿ · vegan options · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** see the map for directions. [View on map ↗](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Th29wHxKmtd1TKGq8)

### Fisherman's Restaurant

One of the island's best-known restaurants, Fisherman's sits by the small pier in Baan Tai with tables close to the water. It is a long-standing favourite for a relaxed seafood dinner as the light fades over the bay.

**Why we love it —** classic beachfront seafood in a setting that has stood the test of time.
**Good to know —** ฿฿ · booking advised at sunset · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** by the pier in Baan Tai, on the south coast. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Fisherman%27s+Restaurant+Koh+Phangan)

## Modern European &amp; Chef's Tables

### DAO by Chef Nir Mesika

The island's benchmark for chef-led dining, set in a beautifully restored Thai house in Thong Sala with just sixteen seats around a communal chef's table. Chef Nir Mesika — who ran restaurants in New York and Tel Aviv before settling here — serves a ten-course tasting menu that folds his Mediterranean and Israeli background into local Thai produce, with several versions for different diets. Hosts Liat and Nir talk you through the thinking behind each dish; guests routinely describe it as a Michelin-level evening they did not expect to find on the island.

**Why we love it —** a ten-course journey where every plate is introduced by the people who made it.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · sixteen seats, reserve well ahead · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** in Thong Sala; see the map for directions. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=DAO+by+Chef+Nir+Mesika+Koh+Phangan)

### CATCH Restaurant & GastroBar

Modern European cooking with an Asian accent, from a French chef with a fine-dining background who often comes out to check on the room himself. Plates are precise and generous — the wagyu tartare is a signature, alongside things like salmon brioche and a proper crêpe Suzette — and the bar turns out serious cocktails. The setting is warm and a little magical, with sea views and even a children's playground, so it works for a date or a family dinner alike.

**Why we love it —** refined, inventive plates and real cocktails, with none of the stiffness.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · reservations recommended for dinner · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** see the map for directions. [View on map ↗](https://maps.app.goo.gl/4B1TJKXWLEhLFdUX9)

### Salt

A polished little seafood restaurant and bar near Thong Sala Beach, built around the freshest catch from the Gulf. Expect tuna tartare and tataki, sea bass, octopus and ceviche, plated with care, and staff who will happily steer you through a wine pairing. Quiet, friendly and stylish — a reliable choice for a date night, with a sunset spot a short walk away.

**Why we love it —** careful seafood and genuinely good wine advice in an easy setting.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · booking advised · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** near Thong Sala Beach; see the map for directions. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Salt+Phangan+restaurant)

### L'Alcove

On Hin Kong Bay, L'Alcove has long been one of the west coast's favourite places to watch the sun go down. The kitchen is French with a Thai twist — duck leg, salmon, a good cheese platter — and the bar runs to French wines and champagne. The real magic is the setting: tables almost on the sand, one of the island's best sunset views, and live music, singers or fire shows on some evenings. It pairs naturally with our guide to [where to watch the sunset in Koh Phangan](journal-where-to-watch-sunset-koh-phangan.html).

**Why we love it —** French cooking, a glass of wine and one of the west coast's finest sunsets.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · arrive before sunset · live music some nights · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** on Hin Kong Bay, west coast. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=L%27Alcove+Koh+Phangan)

## Italian

### NENA Locanda Italiana

Southern Italian cooking run by Mimmo and his partner, and regularly rated among the very best restaurants on the island. The pasta is the draw — a much-praised seafood pasta with generous portions, a simple ricotta, tomato and basil, spaghetti cooked properly al dente, a slow ragù — made with quality ingredients and a genuine welcome. There are gluten-free options too.

**Why we love it —** possibly the best pasta on the island, cooked with real Southern-Italian care.
**Good to know —** ฿฿ · booking advised in high season · gluten-free options · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** in Wok Tum, a few minutes south of Hin Kong on the coastal road. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=NENA+Locanda+Italiana+Koh+Phangan)

### Vertigo Resto Bar

Italian cooking from Chef Roberto, who imports his flour from Italy — it shows in the wood-fired pizza and handmade pasta, alongside fresh seafood like red snapper. A cosy resto, cocktail and wine bar in Haad Rin, run by Roberto and his wife, with fair prices and a warm, relaxed feel.

**Why we love it —** proper Italian pizza and pasta, made with flour brought over from home.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · booking advised · check current hours on Google.
**Getting there —** in the Haad Rin area, south of the island. [View on map ↗](https://share.google/Erf4OqTYRMK9299tU)

## Japanese

### Yukinoya at Anantara Rasananda

Koh Phangan's first and only teppanyaki restaurant, set within the Anantara Rasananda resort on the east coast. Dine at the counter, where chefs turn the grill into a show of knife-work and flames, or outside under the stars overlooking the pool and sea. The kitchen leans farm-to-table and premium — A5 wagyu and Tajima beef, local lobster, delicate hamachi and scallop carpaccio — matched with a deep list of rare sake and inventive drinks like a cucumber-wasabi martini.

**Why we love it —** dinner as an event: a teppanyaki show, wagyu and sea views under the stars.
**Good to know —** ฿฿฿ · reservations recommended · check current hours on Google.

## Mediterranean, Middle Eastern &amp; Moroccan

### BUSTAN

A garden restaurant in the middle of the island, serving an intimate tasting menu just a couple of nights a week. The kitchen is pescatarian and seasonal, changing with the market, with a vegan and vegetarian option prepared for each guest — roughly seven or eight courses that move from Mediterranean flavours into gentle Thai touches, plus homemade cocktails. Diners describe it as a private feast in the jungle: fairy-lit, warm and personal.

**Why we love it —** a seasonal tasting menu in a fairy-lit garden that feels like a private feast.

### Satipot Persian Experience

The island's first authentic Persian restaurant, in Sri Thanu — cushions on the floor, low tables, warm light and music. The kitchen is known for its King's Rice and for slow-cooked lamb, baked under Persian bread with saffron, caramelised onions, raisins and spices, in generous portions. A warm, distinctive change of pace from the island's Thai and Western menus; book a day or two ahead, as it fills most nights.

**Why we love it —** fragrant King's Rice and saffron lamb, in a room made for lingering.

## Food Courts &amp; Night Markets

Some of the island's best eating happens at its markets, where dozens of small stalls gather under one roof or along one street. Expect an easy, buzzing atmosphere — plastic stools, shared tables, the smell of grilling and wok smoke — a mixed crowd of locals, long-stayers and travellers, and prices at the very affordable end (a full plate or a generous street snack for a fraction of restaurant prices). They are the best way to eat well and cheaply, and to graze across several dishes in one evening.

### Thong Sala Night Market (Pantip Night Market)

The island's biggest and best-known market, in the heart of Thong Sala. Stalls run from fragrant curries and grilled skewers to fresh seafood, tropical fruit, Thai desserts and vegan options — busiest in the early evening.

**Why we love it —** the whole island in one place, at everyday prices.
**Good to know —** ฿ · busiest early evening · bring cash. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Thong+Sala+Night+Market+Koh+Phangan)

### Sri Thanu Food Court

A smaller, laid-back food court on the west coast, gathering independent stalls — home to Oh My Burger among others — with a relaxed, yoga-town crowd. A good option for an easy dinner with a group who each want something different.

**Why we love it —** a handful of good stalls and an easy west-coast mood.
**Good to know —** ฿ · casual · check individual stall hours. [View on map ↗](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&amp;query=Sri+Thanu+food+court+Koh+Phangan)

## Best Restaurants by Area

**Hin Kong & Wok Tum (west coast)** — the quiet villages for a slow evening: L'Alcove on the bay for sunset, Dar Mansour for Moroccan slow dining, Mama KOP on the beach, Dear Phangan in its garden, and NENA for Southern Italian.

**Sri Thanu (west coast)** — the island's vegetarian and café heart: Eat.Co, Karma Kafe, Chana Masala, Satipot Persian and the Sri Thanu food court, with Sonolito on the road north.

**Thong Sala** — the best all-rounder for markets and everyday dining: the night market, DAO, Salt, Wasabi, Outlaws Saloon and a Mojo's branch.

**Baan Tai (south)** — best known for beachfront seafood, with Fisherman's by the pier.

**Chaloklum (north), Haad Rin & the east coast** — Mojo's in the northern fishing village, Vertigo for Italian in Haad Rin, and refined resort dining at Yukinoya (Anantara Rasananda) on the east.

## Practical Dining Tips

- **Reserve for the small places.** Chef-led, garden and tasting-menu restaurants fill quickly from December to March and around Full Moon dates.
- **Carry cash.** Markets and smaller kitchens often prefer it; card acceptance varies.
- **Check hours before you go.** Opening times shift with the season — the map link under each restaurant shows current hours.
- **Plan around the sunset.** On the west coast, arrive 30–40 minutes early to catch the light with your table.
- **Getting around.** Most people travel by scooter; distances are short but roads can be dark at night, so ride carefully.

## Final Thoughts

Koh Phangan's best restaurants are not gathered in one place or built around one style of cooking. Part of the pleasure is moving between them — Thai food at the night market one evening, Italian in Wok Tum the next, teppanyaki on the east coast for a celebration, then something slower when the occasion calls for it.
