# Dar Mansour — Backlink Outreach Plan

Living document for building editorial backlinks to darmansour.com. Backlinks
(other reputable sites linking to us) are one of the biggest levers for climbing
Google rankings, especially for a young site.

**Golden rules**
- Always personalise the first line (quote a real detail from their article) — it doubles reply rate.
- Offering a dinner/tasting is the strongest asset: a blogger who dines almost always writes a linked feature.
- Have high-res, frame-free food photos ready to send.
- Always disclose we are the restaurant (transparency = trust with editors and AI engines).
- Track outcomes in the status table at the bottom.

---

## 🤖 AI-visibility note (why we also target big travel guides)

AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude) cite sources
that have (1) high domain authority, (2) structured "things to do / where to eat"
listicles, and (3) many mentions of an entity across the web. For AI, being
**mentioned by name** on these pages matters almost as much as a clickable link:
repeated co-occurrence ("Dar Mansour" + "Moroccan restaurant" + "Koh Phangan")
teaches the models the fact. So each listing/mention in a well-known guide is a
double win — Google SEO **and** AI visibility. This is why Tier 1.5 below is worth
pitching even when a dofollow link isn't guaranteed. (Our on-site foundations for
this — Wikidata, schema, llms.txt, AI-crawler-friendly robots.txt — are already in
place.)

## ✉️ Email template A — Add us to an existing listicle (bloggers / Tier 2)

**Subject:** A Moroccan addition to your Koh Phangan restaurant guide?

Hi [Name],

I really enjoyed your guide to eating on Koh Phangan — it's one of the most genuinely useful ones out there.

I wanted to introduce **Dar Mansour — Morocco's Kitchen**, the island's only Moroccan slow-dining restaurant, near Sri Thanu and Hin Kong. Everything is cooked to order in the traditional way — fire-cooked tajines, hand-rolled couscous, slow-simmered tanjia — in a candlelit space designed to feel like a Moroccan riad.

If it feels like a fit for your readers, I'd be honoured to be included. Happy to send high-res photos, our menu, or host you for dinner if you're ever on the island.

You can see everything here: https://darmansour.com

Warm regards,
Maïja & Bruno — Dar Mansour
